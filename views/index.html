Biological molecules

Biological molecules are the foundation of life, enabling cellular structure, energy production, and genetic inheritance. These molecules are primarily composed of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen, with additional elements like nitrogen, sulfur, or phosphorus in specific cases.

Metabolic Pathways

Metabolic pathways are interconnected networks of chemical reactions that sustain life by converting nutrients into energy and building blocks for cellular components . These pathways are tightly regulated and categorized into two main types: catabolic (breaking down molecules to release energy) and anabolic (using energy to synthesize complex molecules)

Enzyme Kinetics

Enzyme kinetics is the study of the rates and mechanisms of enzyme-catalyzed reactions, providing insights into how enzymes function and how their activity is regulated.
